Why I run

I started running way back when I was 11 years old because Doc said I had to shave a couple pounds lol. At first it was just to live a healthier lifestyle, but then I joined my middle school cross country team where I met some of my bestest friends. I fell in love with the feeling of pounding on the pavement mile after mile. A couple thousand miles later and I'm here running in college. Contrary to popular belief, running cross country is much more than going outside and running for hours. Enjoy the workout schedule our coach gave us for next week!

Weekly mileage:

  • 6 miles at normal effort
  • 1,000 meter repetitions on the track (6 of them)
  • 5 miles at recovery effort
  • 4 mile tempo run, followed by 20 all out hill sprints
  • 8 miles at recovery effort
  • 10-13 LONG RUN (distance depends on fatigue)
  • 1 hour of bike riding at recovery effort
